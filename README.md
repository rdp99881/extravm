## DDoS Protection: Two Layers, Not Just Marketing

Most budget hosts either skip DDoS protection entirely or slap a nominal "mitigation layer" on the specs page that crumbles when anything serious hits. ExtraVM runs a two-layer approach:

**Layer 1** — High-capacity upstream protection partnered with providers like Global Secure Layer (Dallas, LA), ReliableSite (Miami), Royale Hosting (NJ, Amsterdam), and Datapacket (Singapore, Tokyo). These handle volumetric attacks before they hit ExtraVM's network.

**Layer 2** — Their own in-house eBPF/XDP filters deployed at the network edge. This is a modern, low-latency approach that processes traffic at the Linux kernel level — protection that doesn't introduce lag even during an active attack.

There are real-world accounts of TeamSpeak servers on ExtraVM getting hit multiple times with zero downtime and no user-visible degradation. For game server operators who deal with DDoS as a routine annoyance rather than an emergency, this matters.
